Agarwood
Scientific: Aquilaria

Aloeswood & The Resinous Path.

Agarwood is a rare and valuable wood that is formed in the heartwood of Aquilaria trees when they are infected by a fungus. The tree produces a resin that gives agarwood its characteristic aroma, revered for centuries for its fragrance, medicinal properties, and spiritual significance.

The earliest recorded use of agarwood dates back to the Vedas of ancient India (1500-1000 BCE), mentioned as a fragrant offering to the gods and a perfume for the nobility.

Today, agarwood continues to be prized globally. Known as "oud" in China and the Middle East, it is a cornerstone of traditional medicine, luxury perfumery, and spiritual ceremonies.
